Planning first, surgery second
Introduction
Dental implants replace missing teeth using a small titanium post that supports a crown, bridge, or implant-retained denture. Planning matters: before any surgery we assess your mouth, review your medical history, and use CBCT imaging and digital scans to map bone, nerves, bite and the final tooth shape. This allows us to discuss realistic options, timing, and whether additional steps such as bone grafting or sinus lift assessment may be relevant. If implants aren’t the right fit, we’ll explain alternatives such as bridges or removable solutions and help you choose an approach that’s stable, maintainable and comfortable long-term.

=== SECTION: treatment.whatAreImplants ===
Implant basics
What is a dental implant?
A dental implant is a small titanium post that replaces the root of a missing tooth. Once integrated with the bone, we attach a connector (abutment) followed by a crown, bridge, or denture.

Implants sit in living bone near nerves and sinuses. We plan in 3D to assess bone volume and quality, nerve position in the lower jaw, sinus position in the upper jaw, bite forces and spacing, and final tooth position and shape.

=== SECTION: treatment.faq ===
Implant FAQs
Common questions about implants
How long do implants last?
Many implants last for years. Longevity depends on gum health, hygiene, bite forces, and maintenance.
Is implant treatment painful?
Implants are usually placed under local anaesthetic. Post-operative discomfort is typically manageable with standard pain relief.
How long does treatment take?
Some cases are straightforward. Others require staged steps. We will provide a realistic timeline after planning.
Do I need a CBCT scan?
For most implant cases, a CBCT scan is recommended for safe and accurate planning.
Can you place implants if I’ve had a tooth missing for a long time?
Often yes, although bone changes over time. Planning will determine whether grafting or other steps are needed.
What are the alternatives to implants?
Alternatives may include bridges, dentures, or leaving the space in some cases. We will explain the advantages and limitations during consultation.
What if I’m nervous?
We provide step-by-step care and can discuss sedation options where appropriate.
