=== SECTION: treatment.faq ===
Inlay & onlay FAQs
What to know before you proceed
Who is suitable for an inlay or onlay?
They are best for teeth with larger cavities or failing fillings where enough healthy structure remains. We will advise if a crown, repair, or monitoring is safer.
What are the alternatives if I am not suitable?
Depending on the tooth, options may include a bonded filling, a crown for full coverage, or smoothing and monitoring. We explain why each choice would or would not work for your bite.
How long do inlays and onlays last?
Longevity varies with bite forces, material, and cleaning. We choose ceramics or composites suited to your tooth and review the fit at check-ups to catch wear early.
How do I look after the restoration?
Daily cleaning around the margins and attending reviews keeps the tooth and gums healthy. Avoid very hard foods on the tooth while it settles, and let us adjust the bite if it feels high.
What are the risks or limitations?
Restorations can debond or chip if forces are heavy or hygiene is poor. We assess crack lines and grinding habits beforehand and may recommend a protective splint if needed.
How are inlays and onlays priced?
Fees reflect material choice, lab fabrication, and the planning needed for your case. We provide a written estimate before treatment and discuss phased care if appropriate.
