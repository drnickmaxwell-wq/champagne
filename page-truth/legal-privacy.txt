Privacy and your information
Overview
This privacy notice explains how St Mary’s House Dental Care uses and protects personal information.

We are committed to handling personal data responsibly and in line with UK data protection law.

We may collect personal information when you contact the practice, book an appointment, complete forms, or use this website.

This can include contact details, appointment information, and technical data such as IP address and browser type when you use the site.

Personal information is used to provide dental care, manage appointments, communicate with you, and meet our legal and professional obligations.

We do not sell personal data and only share information where necessary for care, administration, or legal reasons.

We take appropriate steps to keep personal information secure, including access controls and secure systems.

Data is kept only for as long as required by clinical, legal, or regulatory obligations.

You have rights under UK data protection law, including the right to access your information and request corrections.

If you have questions about how your data is used, or wish to exercise your rights, please contact the practice.

This privacy notice may be updated from time to time to reflect changes in law or how we operate.

The current version will always be available on this website.

What information we collect
We may collect information you provide when you contact us or book an appointment (for example, name, contact details and appointment-related information). When you use the website, it may also collect limited technical information needed for it to function properly.
Why we use it
We use personal information to manage appointments, respond to enquiries, provide dental care where appropriate, and meet legal and professional obligations.
Who we share it with
We do not sell personal data. We only share information when it’s necessary for care, administration, or legal reasons, and we aim to share the minimum needed.
How long we keep it
Information is kept only for as long as required for clinical, legal or regulatory reasons. Retention can differ depending on what the information relates to.
Your rights
You have rights under UK data protection law, including the right to access your information and request corrections. If you have questions or would like to exercise your rights, please contact the practice.
